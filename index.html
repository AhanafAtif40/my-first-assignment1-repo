Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is best known for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank, which provides small loans to impoverished individuals without requiring collateral. His innovative approach to poverty alleviation has empowered millions, especially women, to achieve financial independence. Born in 1940, Yunus studied economics and earned a Ph.D. from Vanderbilt University. His work has inspired global financial inclusion movements. Despite challenges, he remains a strong advocate for social business and sustainable development. His contributions have reshaped global efforts to eradicate poverty through financial empowerment and entrepreneurship.
